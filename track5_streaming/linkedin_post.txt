Multi-cloud is a trap if you aren't optimizing your egress caching at the TCP socket layer.

Standard egress costs run up to $0.12/GB for GCP, $0.09/GB for AWS S3, and $0.087/GB for Azure Blob. For a system streaming 10 Terabytes daily, that is $900 per day in network tolls.

By pairing ZSTD compression with a cost-aware OrderedDict LRU caching router on persistent TCP sockets, we simulate and prove a 25.52% reduction in net egress costs under strict memory constraints. 

The math:
Baseline Cost: $0.005684 (No ECO Caching)
Realized Cost: $0.004233 (With ECO Caching)
Net Savings: $0.001450 (25.52% Savings)

Scale this to 10 TB/day: it translates to $82,125 saved annually.

Sovereignty begins with cost control. Build your caching routing policies around cloud-specific egress rates, not just access latencies.
